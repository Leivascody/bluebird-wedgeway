Executive Summary

An institutional-quality industrial asset acquired below replacement cost.

Bluebird Commercial Real Estate is acquiring 4061 Wedgeway Court (Suites 4061–4073) — a 43,426 SF multi-tenant industrial property in Earth City, Missouri — for a $3.6M purchase price ($82.90 PSF), meaningfully below replacement cost. The asset is 100% leased to four established tenants with staggered expirations, providing durable cash flow with built-in mark-to-market upside as in-place rents reset toward market over the 10-year hold.

Distribution Waterfall (operating & sale)

  1. 100% to LPs until return of 100% of invested LP capital (return of capital, no preferred return).
  2. Thereafter, 85% to LPs / 15% to the Sponsor (GP) pro-rata on all remaining distributable cash, including sale proceeds.
  3. No catch-up, no tiered promote. A straight 85/15 split after return of capital keeps the economics simple and LP-favorable.

The capex program is defensive — protecting existing NOI and positioning the asset for a competitive Year-10 sale — rather than heavy value-add repositioning. The building is already 100% occupied and functional; dollars are concentrated on exterior/mechanical items that buyers diligence carefully and price into cap rate at exit.
